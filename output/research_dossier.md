## The memory/storage shortage — the core thesis (MU, SNDK, AVGO)

The AI-driven shortage is confirmed across all three memory layers, with order
books demonstrably booked years out:

- **MU (9.0):** FQ2 FY26 record revenue $23.86B (+196% YoY), non-GAAP EPS $12.20
  (+39% beat), record 74.9% gross margin. HBM >30% of DRAM revenue. **HBM
  explicitly sold out through 2026** with customers signing 3–5 year supply
  agreements (a structural break from quarterly pricing); shipped HBM4 12-Hi for
  NVIDIA Vera Rubin in CQ1 2026. FQ3 guide ~$33.5B / ~81% GM. #2 in HBM (~21%,
  passed Samsung) behind SK Hynix (~62%). **Risk: memory cyclicality** — 75-81%
  margins are peak-cycle; FY27 new-fab supply is the wildcard. Next print Jun 24.
- **SNDK (8.5):** FQ3 FY26 revenue $5.95B (+97% sequential), gross margin jumped
  50.9% → 78.4% in one quarter, datacenter revenue +233%. **Doubling enterprise
  3D-NAND prices**; signed **5 multi-year LTAs, 3 worth $42B minimum revenue to
  2031** with >$11B in financial guarantees and take-or-pay clauses — locking
  >⅓ of FY27 bits. FQ4 guide $7.75-8.25B. Highest torque + highest volatility;
  the LTA model is the thesis-defining cyclicality mitigant. #4-5 in NAND.
- **AVGO (7.5):** Q2 FY26 AI revenue $10.8B (+143% YoY), Q3 guided >200% YoY to
  ~$16B. **RPO ~$164.6B**, ~$73B AI backlog, CEO line-of-sight to >$100B AI
  revenue in 2027. ~60% custom-ASIC share (Google/Meta/MSFT/OpenAI/Anthropic),
  ~80% high-end Ethernet. **Risk: customer concentration** — one customer 42% of
  revenue, top-5 ~45%; premium valuation, much already priced.

## Other standouts

- **HWM (9.0):** Q1 26 revenue $2.31B (+19%), adj EPS +42%, EBITDA margin 32%;
  spares +36% to ~$520M; ~15,000-aircraft industry backlog; titanium/superalloy
  vertical integration. Raised 2026 guide. Most acute aero supply gap. Risk:
  OEM build-rate execution; already-strong run.
- **KLAC (8.0):** process-control near-monopoly (~58% share), "unprecedented
  customer urgency securing slots," visibility into 2027; Q3 FY26 rev $3.42B,
  EPS $9.40 beat. Highest-quality semicap moat. Risk: premium valuation, DRAM
  cost/tariff margin pressure.
- **NVDA (9.0):** purest AI-compute shortage, but most-owned/most-priced-in;
  China policy whiplash; hyperscaler custom-ASIC substitution by its customers.
- **BKR (8.0):** turbines sold out through 2028, $33.1B IET backlog, but #3 in
  turbines and half the company is cyclical oilfield services (stigma/cheap).
- **GE / CAT (8.0):** sold-out aero aftermarket ($211B RPO) / record $63B power
  backlog; both quality #1s but more priced-in.
- **LRCX (7.0):** WFE supercycle ($140B), but 34% China revenue + ~45× fwd P/E.
- **LLY (6.0):** dominant GLP-1 demand, but FDA declared the shortage *resolved*
  — scarcity rent fading.
- **NEM (5.0):** #1 gold, record FCF, but the "shortage" is a price cycle it
  doesn't control; production declining 2026.
