## Per-name theses (top 5)

**1. MSFT — Microsoft.** The only name all four lenses ranked #1 — a first for
this funnel. The dip (~31% off the high, worst start to a year since 2000) is
driven by the ~$190B CY2026 capex guide compressing quarterly FCF to $15.8B
against $31.8B net income; every demand metric accelerated through it (Azure
+40%, AI run-rate $37B +123%, RPO $627B +99% — all verified from the Apr 29
FY26 Q3 release). Temporary because capex is a choice and backlog is a
contract; the moat (enterprise switching costs + owning the AI on-ramp) is the
one AI strengthens rather than threatens. Catalyst: FY26 Q4 print late July,
then quarterly RPO conversion. See `final_pick.md` for the full build.

**2. IDXX — Idexx Laboratories.** The consensus quality pick: ranked by all
four ballots, top-3 in three of them, on the highest irreplaceability score of
all 15 (8.5) — a razor/razorblade installed base plus a physical reference-lab
network an LLM literally cannot route around, with only one material
competitor. The dip cause (US vet visits −1% in Q1'26) is the definition of
cyclical, and management answered it by *raising* FY26 guidance to $4.68-4.76B
revenue / $14.45-14.90 EPS on +11% organic recurring diagnostics (verified,
May 2026 8-K). Catalyst: inVue Dx ramp (5,500 placements targeted 2026 —
verified; 1,100 already placed in Q1) plus visit normalization. Caveat: at
~34x forward, its EV upside sits below the +15% guardrail (see scenarios) —
quality is the margin of safety here, not the multiple.

**3. CME — CME Group.** The prior run's #1, still a top-3 moat (liquidity
network effect + CCP netting AI cannot replicate) that printed a record Q1
(revenue $1.88B +14%, adj EPS $3.36 +20%, ADV 36.2M +22% — verified) into a
~33% fear-driven drawdown (Duffy succession to Lynne Fitzpatrick March 2027 +
CFTC-approved Kalshi Bitcoin perpetuals — both verified). The honest update:
the market has begun agreeing — CME re-rated from ~$231 (07-02 pick) to ~$273
by Jul 10, so roughly half the originally modeled upside has been realized.
It stays #3 on moat + momentum of proof, but new money gets a thinner entry
than the screen snapshot implies.

**4. VEEV — Veeva Systems.** Agent B's single pick. A net-cash vertical-SaaS
standard still compounding 16% (FY27 guided $3.59-3.60B, above estimates) that

while IQVIA's 2029 CRM sunset *forces* the industry to re-platform, and

the natural destination. At ~19-20x forward vs a ~72x 10-year average, it is
the most-de-rated quality name in the set. Trap caveat: the thesis requires
actually winning the 2026-2029 migration window against a maturing Salesforce
