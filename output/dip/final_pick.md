## One-paragraph thesis

S&P Global is two **uncopyable regulatory toll-booths** bolted together — the
Moody's/S&P credit-**ratings duopoly** (NRSRO-gated: essentially every dollar of
new bond issuance must buy a rating) and the **S&P 500 index-licensing monopoly**
(every fund that tracks the index pays a royalty) — plus a fast-growing
private-credit data business (+25%). It has fallen ~27% off its high and trades
at ~18.5x forward earnings versus a **~40x five-year average** — roughly the
**10th percentile of its own valuation history** — *not* because anything in the
franchise broke, but because of a cyclical bond-issuance trough, soft Market
Intelligence, and an overhang from a corporate spinoff that **completes July 1,
2026**. None of those touch the toll-booth. As issuance re-accelerates (consensus
+4.8% in 2026), margins step from ~51% toward ~53%, and buybacks compound, a
multiple that simply normalizes part-way back toward history delivers the
re-rate. This is the cleanest "right business, wrong price, dated unlock" setup on
the entire 14-name shortlist.

## Why the dip is temporary, not permanent

- **The cause is cyclical + mechanical, not structural.** Ratings revenue softened
  because *bond-issuance volumes* were low in a higher-rate window — and issuance
  is **already inflecting positively (+4.8% projected for 2026)**. A ratings
  duopoly's revenue dipping on low volumes is the canonical temporary dip:
  volumes mean-revert; the toll-booth doesn't move.
- **The franchise is unchanged.** The duopoly with Moody's is intact, pricing
  power is intact, and the S&P 500 index brand is unchallenged. The drop is
  almost entirely **multiple compression**, not an earnings collapse.
- **The AI fear is backwards.** Independent analysts (Morningstar, Redburn)
  conclude AI is *"redistribution, not disruption"* and **strengthens** the moat —
  because a regulated NRSRO rating is precisely the output a language model is
  legally barred from producing, and proprietary regulated datasets gain value as
  analytics proliferate.
- **A dated catalyst removes the overhang.** The Mobility (auto-data) spinoff
  **completes July 1, 2026**, de-risking the story into a higher-margin pure-play.

## Why it's the category leader / why the moat is intact (and AI-proof)

- **Ratings:** one of two NRSRO-designated agencies (with Moody's). Issuing a
  regulated credit rating requires a government designation — a startup *or an AI
  model* legally cannot do it. This is regulatory/certification lock-in, the
  hardest moat type.
- **Indices:** S&P **owns the S&P 500 brand**. Every index fund/ETF tracking it
  pays a licensing royalty — a pure toll on the entire passive-investing boom. No
  model can mint or relicense the brand.
- **Private-credit data:** a deepening tollbooth growing +25%, riding the
  fastest-growing corner of credit.
- **The only AI-exposed sliver** is Market Intelligence / Capital IQ Pro (a
  minority segment where cheaper analytics could nibble margin) — and the dip's
  discount more than prices that in.
