## Per-name thesis (top 5)

**1. MU — Micron Technology.** Entire CY2026 HBM output sold out under binding multi-year
contracts (16 Strategic Capacity Agreements, &gt;$100B committed value), inside the worst DRAM
shortage in ~15 years (supplier inventory at 2018-supercycle lows). Micron is #3 of a 3-firm
leading-edge DRAM oligopoly (~90% combined share) — not the category leader, but a scaled,
qualification-locked member of a club no hyperscaler can build its way into. Irreplaceability is
the strongest argument in the whole dossier: DRAM/HBM fabs cost $15–20B+ and take years to
qualify, so unlike NVDA or AVGO there is no in-sourcing path around Micron. At 6.5x forward
earnings with +52% analyst upside, it is also the cheapest name in the entire research set.

**2. AVGO — Broadcom.** The best-documented backlog in the dossier: $162B total, $73B
AI-specific, with a reaffirmed $56B FY2026 AI target stepping to $100B+ FY2027. Its position is
genuinely two-sided — Broadcom is the *vehicle* by which hyperscalers in-source custom silicon
away from NVDA (a bull case for AVGO), but the same dynamic means its own named customers
(Google, Meta, OpenAI, and others) could eventually take design fully in-house, which is why
this ranking flags rather than vetoes it. Elite SerDes/packaging IP and seven generations of
Google TPU co-design provide real near-term stickiness; it is also the priciest of the top 3
on forward earnings (20.6x).

**3. APH — Amphenol.** The cleanest order-book confirmation of a live AI-interconnect shortage:
record $9.4B Q1 2026 orders, book-to-bill 1.24:1, IT-datacom organic growth +81%. #2 in the
global connector market and closing on #1. The clearest long-term disintermediation threat —
co-packaged optics eventually displacing copper interconnects — is directly hedged via the
$10.5B CommScope CCS acquisition, which gives Amphenol its own optics roadmap rather than
leaving it exposed to being routed around. Best analyst-upside cushion (+16%) of the non-MU
finalists, though the EV math below falls just short of the +15% single-pick guardrail — real
thesis, thinner margin of safety.

**4. HWM — Howmet Aerospace.** The picks-and-shovels play on the same jet-engine shortage as
GE: single-crystal turbine-airfoil casting is a tight oligopoly-to-duopoly, and every airfoil is
qualified to a specific engine program, making requalification to a new supplier multi-year and
risk-laden. Spares revenue surged 36% to ~$520M at a record 32.0% EBITDA margin in Q1 2026,
mirroring the aftermarket shortage powering GE. Offers a meaningfully better valuation cushion
than GE (+14% vs. +4% to mean target) for a comparably strong moat.

**5. GE — GE Aerospace.** The single highest irreplaceability score in the dossier (9/10):
CFM (50/50 with Safran) is sole-source on the entire Boeing 737 MAX and on the 777/777X,
powering roughly 3 of every 4 commercial flights, with a certification wall that makes
in-sourcing essentially impossible for any airline. Backlog is enormous (~$190–210B, with 95%
of Q2 spare-parts revenue already locked in) and spare-parts orders accelerated ~40% in a
60-day window. The honest caveat: this is also the most fully priced name of the finalists —
only +4% to the screen's mean target, though several fresh analyst price-target hikes (UBS
$426, Jefferies $455, Citi $431) suggest that mean may be stale.

**6–10 (one line each):** **LRCX** — memory/HBM etch-deposition oligopoly leader, but full
valuation and China (MATCH Act) risk cap the upside. **CDNS** — the most irreplaceable business
model in the dossier (EDA duopoly toll booth, best backlog visibility at $8B), but a
consumption-growth compounder rather than a physical-shortage bet. **KLAC** — the strongest
pure moat in the whole research set (near-monopoly in process control), undermined by trading
*above* its own mean analyst target. **NVDA** — still the AI-compute standard with the best
software moat (CUDA), but also the name most exposed to the doctrine's central trap as ASIC
in-sourcing grows ~44.6% vs. ~16.1% merchant-GPU growth. **AMAT** — broadest, cheapest
semi-equipment exposure, but the most price-extended chart in the group plus a fresh $252M
China export-control penalty.

**Where they agreed:** MU and AVGO appeared in the top 3 of all four ballots (MU: ranks

## Risk lens & leverage-safety note

**Sector concentration.** 7 of the top 10 sit in semiconductors/semi-equipment (MU, AVGO,
LRCX, CDNS, KLAC, NVDA, AMAT) — a memory-pricing rollover or an AI-capex pause would hit most of
this ranking simultaneously, not just one name. HWM and GE (aerospace) and APH (interconnects,
itself AI-capex-linked) provide only partial diversification within the list.

**Highest-risk vs. steadier compounders.** MU and, further down, SNDK are the highest-beta
names — genuine memory cyclicals that have historically drawn down 50%+ peak-to-trough (2018,
2022). CDNS and GE are the closest to steady compounders in this list (subscription backlog,
certification-locked aftermarket annuity respectively) but both still carry real cyclical
exposure (semiconductor design spend, air-travel demand) and neither is immune to a drawdown.

**Drawdown reality.** Every bear case in the scenario table above assumes a 30–44% decline
within 12–18 months, consistent with this group's historical cyclicality — this is not a
low-volatility basket.

**Leverage (education, not a recommendation):** given the correlated sector concentration and
historical drawdown magnitude across this list, any use of leverage would compound losses across
*multiple* positions simultaneously in a shared downturn (e.g., an AI-capex pause), not just one.
This system is cash-only by policy; this is risk education, not a leverage suggestion.

## Thesis-break / trap triggers to watch across the list

- **NVDA (highest trap exposure):** any acceleration in hyperscaler custom-ASIC share beyond
  the current ~44.6% growth trajectory, or a named hyperscaler publicly shifting a training
  workload off NVIDIA hardware.
- **AVGO:** a named XPU customer (Google, Meta, OpenAI, etc.) announcing it is bringing chip
  design fully in-house and dropping Broadcom as co-design partner.
- **MU / memory group broadly:** DRAM/HBM ASPs decelerating or rolling over sequentially;
  any renegotiation or cancellation of Micron's Strategic Capacity Agreements.
- **KLAC:** already trading above its mean target — any earnings miss against elevated
  expectations has little valuation cushion to absorb it.
- **AMAT / LRCX:** further escalation of China semiconductor export controls (AMAT already
  paid a $252M penalty; Lam is named in the proposed MATCH Act).
- **GE / HWM:** a slowdown in air-travel demand or a delivery-ramp/certification setback that
  breaks the "95% of spare-parts revenue already in backlog" visibility.
